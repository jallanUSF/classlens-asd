# Lesson Plan: Raptor Greetings: A Mission with Blue

**Student:** Maya (Grade 3, ASD Level 2)  
**Student ID:** Maya_G3_ASD2  
**Date:** April 4, 2026  
**Duration:** 30 Minutes  

---

## 🎯 Lesson Overview
**Goal Alignment:** `comm_greeting_01`  
**Objective:** Maya will initiate or respond to peer greetings (verbal or gesture-based) with 80% accuracy, aligning with her communication IEP goal.  
**Interest Integration:** This lesson leverages Maya's affinity for *Jurassic World*, specifically the character Blue the Raptor, her preference for the color purple, and her strength in counting.

---

## 🛠 Materials Needed
*   **Visual & Social Supports:** 
    *   Small figure of Blue the Raptor
    *   Purple 3x5 index cards with sight words (**Hi**, **Hello**, **Hey**) in bold black ink
    *   "Raptor Greeting Mission" checklist (Purple sheet with 3 peer name spaces and dinosaur-shaped checkboxes)
*   **Sensory Tools:**
    *   Weighted lap pad
    *   Noise-canceling headphones (available as needed)
*   **Reinforcers:**
    *   Dinosaur stickers

---

## 📝 Instructional Sequence

### 1. Warm-Up: Sensory Grounding & Activation
*   **Sensory Start:** Begin the session with Maya using her weighted lap pad to provide calming sensory input.
*   **Concept Activation:** The teacher will present a photo of Blue the Raptor "greeting" another dinosaur. 
*   **Engagement Question:** Ask Maya, *"What is Blue saying?"* to introduce and activate the concept of social greetings.

### 2. Guided Practice: Modeling the Mission
*   **Social Modeling:** The teacher and a peer will demonstrate a "Raptor Greeting" using a purple visual cue card.
*   **Supported Practice:** Maya will practice responding to a peer's greeting. The teacher will provide the following prompts as needed:
    *   **Verbal Prompt:** *"You can say Hi!"*
    *   **Gesture Prompt:** Pointing to the purple visual card.

### 3. Independent Practice: The Greeting Mission
*   **Activity:** Maya is assigned the "Raptor Greeting Mission." 
*   **Execution:** Using her purple checklist, Maya will navigate the classroom to visit three designated peers.
*   **Task:** Maya must either initiate a greeting or respond to one from the peer.
*   **Counting Integration:** Maya will mark a tally/checkbox for each successful interaction, appealing to her love of counting.

---

## 📉 Assessment & Data Collection
**Method:** Tally Sheet  
**Procedure:** The teacher will record a "Yes" or "No" for each initiation or response across 10 trials.  
**Success Metric:**  
$$\text{Accuracy} = \left( \frac{\text{Successful Greetings}}{10} \right) \times 100$$

---

## 🧩 Scaffolding & Accommodations
| Strategy | Implementation |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Wait Time** | Provide a minimum of 3 seconds of processing time before offering a prompt. |
| **Sensory Regulation** | If Maya shows signs of being overwhelmed, immediately offer the weighted lap pad. |
| **Cognitive Load Reduction** | Use the purple sight-word cards as visual anchors to reduce the demand of spontaneous speech generation. |
| **Environmental Support** | Noise-canceling headphones are available if the classroom volume becomes a barrier. |
