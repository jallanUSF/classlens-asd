## Goal Alignment
This schedule is designed to embed natural opportunities for Jaylen to utilize his AAC device to request needs, specifically focusing on sensory regulation, activity choice, and teacher attention. This directly supports his IEP goal of achieving **80% accuracy in requesting wants and needs**.

## Implementation Notes for Staff

### Sensory Considerations
*   **Visuals:** Use the muted palette strictly to prevent overstimulation. Ensure the final product is matte to avoid glare, as Jaylen is sensory-avoidant to bright lights.
*   **Tactile:** Use soft-touch Velcro for all icons to avoid "scratchy" textures that may cause sensory distress.

### AAC Integration & Prompting
To foster independence and avoid prompt dependency, please follow this hierarchy:
1.  **Visual Cue:** Point to the AAC Icon on the schedule.
2.  **Wait Time:** Provide a 5–10 second processing window for Jaylen to initiate the request.
3.  **Prompting:** If no response occurs, utilize a **partial physical prompt** (guiding his hand toward the device). 
4.  **Avoid:** Avoid high-pitched verbal prompts, as these may be overstimulating.

### Interest Weaving
*   **Engagement:** Use "station" terminology and the reward of Gordon and Percy to maintain motivation.
*   **Transitions:** The "Find number 5" step leverages Jaylen's specific interest in that numeral to create a positive and successful transition point into his morning work.
