## Starting My Day

Every morning, I arrive at school. My classmates arrive at school at the same time. We all gather in the classroom or the hallway to start our day. I can think of school arrival as the first stop on my daily map.

## Understanding Greetings

Sometimes, my friends see me and say "Hi" or "Good morning." These words are called greetings. Greetings are like the first few presidents in a sequence; they start the conversation for the day. When a friend greets me, it means they are happy to see me. I can categorize these words as "friendly greetings."

## How I Can Respond

There are many different ways to respond to a greeting. I can say "Hi," "Hello," or "Good morning" back to my peer. My friends feel glad when I respond to them. It lets them know that I heard them and that I am being friendly. I can choose a response that feels comfortable for me.

## My Quiet Moments

Some days, I might feel quiet, like a calm map of a peaceful place. It is okay to feel quiet sometimes. A small wave or a smile is also a way to respond to a greeting. Friends usually understand when someone is having a quiet morning. I can use a wave or a smile if I do not want to use words.

## Navigating My Social Map

Responding to greetings helps me connect with my classmates. When we greet each other, it makes the classroom feel welcoming for everyone. It is okay to take a moment to think before I respond. I am learning how to navigate my social map every day.
