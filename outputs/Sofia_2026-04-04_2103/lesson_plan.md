# Lesson Plan: Mapping Social Connections: Moving Beyond the Facts

**Student:** Sofia (Grade 5, ASD Level 1)  
**Date:** April 4, 2026  
**Student ID:** Sofia_G5_ASD1  
**Duration:** 45 Minutes

---

## 🎯 Lesson Objective
**Goal ID:** `social_communication_initiation_01`  
Sofia will initiate social conversations with peers that move beyond fact-sharing by utilizing "Connection Questions" (inquiring about opinions, feelings, or preferences) with 80% consistency, aligning with her social communication IEP goal.

## 🧩 Interest Integration
To leverage Sofia's strengths in organization and her interest in geography and history, this lesson frames social interactions as a structured system. The activities utilize a "Conversation Map" (geography theme) and "The President's Social Protocol" (US Presidents theme) to categorize and organize social exchanges.

---

## 🛠️ Materials & Support

### Required Materials
*   **Conversation Map:** 11x17 printed flowchart illustrating the path: *Observation $\rightarrow$ Fact $\rightarrow$ Connection Question*.
*   **Topic Cards:** Set of 10 cards featuring geography and history imagery (e.g., the Periodic Table, the Andes Mountains, the White House).
*   **Social Protocol Checklist:** A formal, document-style checklist for Sofia to track and verify her successes.
*   **Sensory Tools:** Fidget ring for rhythmic tapping during transitions.
*   **Timing:** Timer for structured transitions between activities.

### Scaffolding & Accommodations
*   **Visual Aids:** Provide a "Cheat Sheet" of connection-starter phrases (e.g., *"What do you think about...?", "How do you feel about...?", "Do you prefer...?"*).
*   **Sensory Regulation:** Allow the use of the fidget ring during the "Observation" phase to support regulation.
*   **Environmental Support:** Ensure the learning environment is quiet, predictable, and free of unexpected disruptions.

---

## 📈 Instructional Sequence

### 1. Warm-Up: The Categorization Challenge
Sofia will be presented with five statements (e.g., *"Washington was the first president"* vs. *"I love reading history books"*). She will quickly sort these into two piles: **"Fact"** or **"Personal Connection."** This engages her preference for organization and prepares her for the lesson's conceptual divide.

### 2. Guided Practice: Navigating the Map
Using the **Conversation Map** visual flowchart, the teacher and Sofia will practice the transition from a "Fact Statement" to a "Connection Question." The teacher will model how to move through the flowchart to ensure the transition feels logical and systemic.

### 3. Main Activity: The Bridging Technique
Sofia will categorize a series of statements into "Fact-Sharing" (Data) and "Connection-Building" (Social). She will then practice "bridging" a fact to a social question.
*   **Example:** *"I see you have a map of Italy [Fact]. Have you ever wanted to travel there? [Connection]."*

### 4. Independent Practice: Peer Interview Quest
Sofia will be provided with a list of three peers and a "Map" of topics. Her task is to initiate a conversation with each peer, starting with a factual observation and successfully bridging to a social connection question.

---

## ✅ Assessment & Evaluation

**Success Criteria:**  
The teacher will observe and record whether Sofia asks a follow-up question regarding a peer's feeling or opinion rather than a factual detail.

| **Incorrect (Fact-Based)** | **Correct (Connection-Based)** |
| :--- | :--- |
| *"Which state is that in?"* | *"Do you like visiting there?"* |
| *"What year did that happen?"* | *"How do you feel about that part of history?"* |
