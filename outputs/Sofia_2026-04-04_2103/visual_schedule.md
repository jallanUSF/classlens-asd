# Visual Schedule: Morning Arrival Routine
**Student:** Sofia  
**Grade:** 5 (ASD Level 1)  
**Date:** April 4, 2026  

---

## 🎯 Goal Alignment
This schedule is designed to facilitate a structured morning arrival routine while embedding targeted opportunities for Sofia to practice her IEP goal: **initiating social conversations with peers using evidence-based strategies (asking questions and showing interest).**

---

## 🛠 Design Specifications
*To be used for physical creation of the visual aid.*

| Feature | Specification |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Dimensions** | 8.5" x 11", Portrait orientation |
| **Layout** | Six large, distinct vertical rectangles separated by bold downward arrows ($\downarrow$) |
| **Visual Palette** | Muted tones (Sage Green, Soft Blue, Cream) to minimize sensory overstimulation |
| **Physical Build** | Laminated; 0.5" white space between rectangles to accommodate Velcro "check-off" strips |

---

## 📅 Morning Arrival Schedule

**Step 1: Unpack and Store**  
*Icon: Simple line-drawing of a backpack*  
**Action:** I will put my bag in my cubby and take out my folder.  
$\downarrow$

**Step 2: Organize Space**  
*Icon: Neatly organized set of three folders/bins*  
**Action:** I will arrange my desk materials in my favorite order.  
*(Purpose: Grounding sensory transition)*  
$\downarrow$

**Step 3: Pick "Chat Topic"**  
*Icon: Small world map with a speech bubble*  
**Action:** I will look at the "Topic of the Day" card.  
*Example: “Which state has the coolest shape?” or “Which president had the most interesting pet?”*  
$\downarrow$

**Step 4: Start a Conversation**  
*Icon: Two silhouette figures with a small bridge connecting them*  
**Action:** I will find a peer and use my topic to say hello.  
*Example: "Hi [Name], I was thinking about the map of Ohio. Do you like maps?"*  
$\downarrow$

**Step 5: Ask a Question**  
*Icon: Large, friendly question mark*  
**Action:** I will ask my friend a question about them to show interest.  
*Example: "What is your favorite thing to do on weekends?"*  
$\downarrow$

**Step 6: Presidential Bonus**  
*Icon: Gold seal/Presidential Medal*  
**Action:** I earn 3 minutes of "Presidential Research" time or map-categorizing before the morning bell.

## 👩‍🏫 Teacher Implementation Notes

### Sensory & Environmental Supports
*   **Low-Traffic Zones:** To mitigate anxiety regarding crowded or noisy spaces, ensure the "Start a Conversation" step occurs in a designated low-traffic area of the classroom.
*   **Social Pairing:** Consider pairing Sofia with a pre-selected "social buddy" to lower the barrier for initiation.

### Instructional Scaffolding
*   **Sentence Starters:** Since Sofia is an advanced reader, the "Chat Topic" cards should provide specific sentence starters to bridge the gap between sharing a fact and initiating a social exchange. 
    *   *Example: "I noticed you are wearing a blue shirt; is that your favorite color?"*

### Reinforcement Strategy
*   **High-Value Interest:** The "Presidential Bonus" leverages Sofia's specific interest in US Presidents and categorization. This serves as a powerful positive reinforcer for completing the social requirements of the routine.
