        # Fire Drill Observation — Jaylen (04/08, 10:15 AM)

        **Student:** Jaylen T.  
**Event:** Unannounced fire drill (Building A)  
**Date:** 2026-04-08  
**Duration:** 6 minutes (alarm) + 4 minutes (staging)  
**Observers:** Mrs. Park (SPED), Keisha (1:1)  
**Related IEP Goals:** G2 (tolerates loud transitions)  

        ---

        **Pre-teaching done:** Fire drill social story read 2x this week.
        Jaylen had been told "a drill is coming this week" each morning.

        **10:15** — Alarm sounds. Jaylen was working on a Thomas puzzle.
        First reaction: freezes, both hands go to ears. Vocalization: short
        whine (~2 seconds).

        **10:16** — Keisha kneels next to him, holds up the "fire drill"
        PECS card from her lanyard and says "Jaylen. Fire drill. We walk
        outside. Then we come back." Jaylen looks at the card, then at her.
        He does not move.

        **10:16:30** — Keisha offers Jaylen his weighted lap cat (Thomas-themed
        weighted plush). He grabs it and stands up.

        **10:17** — Jaylen walks to the door holding Keisha's hand and the
        weighted cat. Passes through hallway without pulling away. Ears are
        still covered with one hand.

        **10:18** — Outside at staging area. Jaylen rocks in place, hums
        softly (self-regulation pattern we've seen before). No elopement, no
        crying, no aggression.

        **10:20** — All-clear. Walking back in. Jaylen drops the weighted
        cat once. Picks it back up on verbal prompt.

        **10:22** — Back in classroom. Within 90 seconds, returns to Thomas
        puzzle. Finishes it. No further distress.

        ---

        **Adult redirects needed:** **1** (weighted cat pickup)
        **Goal target:** <2 redirects → **MET**

        **Major win:** This is the first fire drill this year where Jaylen
        did not cry, elope, or require physical guidance through the hallway.
        Last drill (02/12) required 2 staff escorts and he did not settle
        for 20+ min after returning. Today's recovery was <2 min.

        **Team hypothesis:** Combination of (a) social story pre-teaching,
        (b) weighted Thomas cat as regulation tool, (c) consistent 1:1
        language during the event.

        **Next step:** Try one unannounced drill without pre-warning (with
        weighted cat still available) to probe generalization.
