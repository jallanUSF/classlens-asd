        **Prompt (given by Ms. Delgado):** "Pick one character from your book.
        What do they want? What's stopping them? What does this tell you
        about who they are as a person?"

        **Amara's response (handwritten, 1.5 pages, transcribed):**

        > I'm writing about Shoto Todoroki. What Todoroki wants on the
        > surface is to be a hero using only his ice powers, not his fire.
        > He wants this because his fire came from his father Endeavor, who
        > was abusive to his family. Todoroki thinks that if he uses his
        > fire he's 'becoming his father,' so he refuses.
        >
        > But underneath that, what he really wants is to prove he isn't
        > his father. That's different from not using the fire. Deku is
        > the one who points this out during the Sports Festival fight.
        > Deku basically tells him 'your power is YOUR power, not his.'
        > That's when Todoroki starts to use his fire for the first time
        > in years.
        >
        > What's stopping him isn't his father directly — Endeavor isn't
        > even at the fight. What's stopping him is the promise he made to
        > his mother as a little kid. His mom told him he could be a hero
        > on his own terms. But she also told him not to let his father's
        > hatred poison him. Todoroki is trying to honor BOTH things and
        > they don't fit together.
        >
        > This tells me Todoroki is someone who takes promises really
        > seriously, maybe too seriously. He's loyal but the loyalty is
        > complicated. It also tells me he needed someone OUTSIDE his
        > family to give him permission to use his own power. That's why
        > Deku matters to his arc — Deku isn't family so his opinion
        > doesn't carry the same weight of guilt.
        >
        > I think the lesson here is that sometimes the thing we're told
        > about ourselves when we're little becomes a cage, even if the
        > person who told us meant well.
