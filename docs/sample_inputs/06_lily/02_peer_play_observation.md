        # Recess Observation — Lily (04/09)

        **Student:** Lily J.

**Setting:** 4th grade recess (playground + sandbox + blacktop)  
**Observer:** Ms. Okonkwo (SPED)  
**Duration:** 25 min  
**Related IEP Goals:** G2 (peer collaborative play) — RECOVERING  

        ---

        **Background:** Lily had a rough February after a bad sub day
        (12 peer play observations in a row with zero collaboration). Since
        then we've been building recovery slowly. She's allowed to bring
        her 'ocean notebook' to recess as a bridge tool — peers can come
        look at it with her.

        **Recess today:**

        **12:45** — Lily walks to the sandbox area. Sits on the edge.
        Opens ocean notebook to the narwhal page (she's been drawing
        narwhals this week).

        **12:48** — Peer (Priya) sits down next to Lily. "Are you drawing
        more narwhals?" Lily: "Yeah. This one has scars because old
        narwhals get scars from ice." Priya: "Wait really?" Lily: "Yeah."
        **[collaborative exchange #1 — text-based but engaged]**

        **12:50** — Peer (Jamie) joins. "Can I see?" Lily hands Jamie the
        notebook. Jamie flips through. Lily watches anxiously but doesn't
        snatch. Jamie says "the orcas are the best." Lily: "They're my
        favorite too." **[collaborative exchange #2 — sharing a valued
        object without distress is huge for Lily]**

        **12:54** — Priya suggests: "Do you want to build an ocean in
        the sandbox? Like a map?" Lily: [pauses for ~4 seconds] "...okay."
        **[Lily agreeing to shift from notebook to physical collaborative
        play — this is the recovery target]**

        **12:56** — Priya, Jamie, and Lily start building. Lily takes charge
        of the "deep ocean" zone. Priya does the reef. Jamie does the
        shore. Occasional disagreements about where to put the kelp.
        Lily uses her ocean notebook as a "reference book."

        **1:02** — Jamie accidentally knocks over Lily's kelp forest.
        Lily's shoulders go up — early dysregulation cue. Before Ms.
        Okonkwo can move to support, Lily says: "Jamie, don't do that.
        That was my kelp." Jamie: "Sorry, I'll help rebuild." Lily: "Okay."
        **[This is self-advocacy + regulation — previously she'd have
        shut down or cried.]**

        **1:10** — Bell. Lily helps put the "ocean" away with Priya.
        Walks back to class with both peers. Smiles twice.
