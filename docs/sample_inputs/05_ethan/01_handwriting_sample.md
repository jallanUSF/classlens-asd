        # Fine Motor Work Sample — Ethan (04/09)

        **Student:** Ethan P.  
**Date:** 2026-04-09  
**Task:** Name + 5-word weather sentence  
**Tool:** Standard #2 pencil, no grip  
**Related IEP Goals:** G2 (fine motor / handwriting) — PLATEAU  

        ---

        **Task:** Ethan was asked to (1) write his name and (2) copy the
        sentence "The wind is very strong today" from the whiteboard. He
        was given lined primary paper (the kind with a solid bottom line,
        dotted middle line, and solid top line).

        **Teacher transcription of what Ethan actually produced:**

        Line 1 (name attempt):
        > E t h a n
        — Letters present but sized inconsistently.
          The E is ~2x the height of the other letters.
          The 'h' descender goes below the solid bottom line.
          No spacing between letters — "Ethan" runs together.
          Pencil pressure: heavy (tore paper slightly on the 'E').

        Line 2 (copied sentence):
        > T h e   w i     s   v r y
        — Ethan started the sentence but stopped partway.
          Missed letters in "wind" (wrote "wi") and "strong" (didn't attempt).
          Spacing between words exists but is inconsistent.
          Stopped writing after "vry" and said "all done."

        **Time on task:** 9 minutes (asked for 5 minutes of work).
        **Erasures:** 7 (mostly re-trying the lowercase 'n').
        **Prompts needed:** 3 verbal, 1 hand-over-hand on the 'n'.

        **Teacher observation (Mrs. Lee):**
        Ethan's handwriting has been flat for about 6 weeks. He plateaued
        at the same letter-formation quality we saw in early March. He is
        NOT regressing — his outputs are consistent — but he's not improving
        either. He also fatigues fast: after about 8 minutes of writing he
        starts to complain or disengage.

        **Current G2 IEP target:** "Write a 5-word sentence with
        letters within the lined guides, 4 of 5 trials."
        **Current performance:** 1-2 of 5 trials for the last 6 weeks.
