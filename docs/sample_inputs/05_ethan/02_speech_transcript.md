        # Speech Sample — Ethan (04/08, morning circle)

        **Student:** Ethan P.  
**Date:** 2026-04-08  
**Setting:** Morning circle, 2nd grade (15 students + teacher)  
**Recorded by:** Ms. Rivera (SLP)  
**Related IEP Goals:** G1 (functional communication beyond echolalia)  

        ---

        **Context on Ethan's language:** Ethan is echolalic — he often
        repeats phrases he's heard recently, especially from YouTube
        (ceiling fan reviews, weather reporters) and TV. He does have
        spontaneous language but it's buried. This transcript is tagged
        with [ECHO], [FUNC] (functional/spontaneous), or [MIXED].

        **9:02 — Morning circle begins. Topic: weather.**

        **Teacher:** "Good morning! Ethan, what's the weather like today?"
        **Ethan:** "Today we're tracking a powerful nor'easter that's
        developing off the coast." [ECHO — this is a Jim Cantore weather
        channel line, heard repeatedly at home per mom]

        **Teacher:** [smiling] "Wow, you sound like a meteorologist! Can
        you look outside and tell me what YOU see right now?"
        **Ethan:** [walks to window, looks for 4 seconds] "It's... windy.
        The flag is doing the thing." [FUNC — spontaneous, novel, uses
        a workaround ("doing the thing") for vocabulary gap]

        **Teacher:** "Yes! The flag is flapping. Can you say 'the flag
        is flapping'?"
        **Ethan:** "The flag is flapping." [ECHO — repeated prompt]
        **Ethan:** [pause] "It's really flapping." [FUNC — novel
        extension with intensifier]

        **9:07 — Free talk turn.**

        **Peer (Jaden):** "I saw a rainbow yesterday."
        **Ethan:** [5-second pause] "Rainbows happen when sunlight refracts
        through water droplets suspended in the atmosphere after
        precipitation." [ECHO — this is from a science video]
        **Peer (Jaden):** "...cool?"
        **Teacher:** "That's really interesting, Ethan! Did YOU see a
        rainbow yesterday?"
        **Ethan:** "No. I saw a ceiling fan video. It was the Hunter
        Original, 52 inch, five blade." [FUNC — declining the premise
        and self-correcting to what he actually did]

        **9:11 — Transition to tables.**

        **Teacher:** "Okay, everyone stand up and go to tables."
        **Ethan:** [remains seated] "Go to tables." [ECHO]
        **Teacher:** "Ethan, that means YOU. Stand up please."
        **Ethan:** [stands] "I'm going to the table." [FUNC — self-narration]

        ---

        **Sample analysis (Ms. Rivera):**

        - Total utterances: 9
        - Pure echolalia: 3 (33%)
        - Functional/spontaneous: 4 (44%)
        - Mixed (echo with modification): 2 (22%)

        **6-week trend on functional %:**
        28% → 31% → 35% → 38% → 40% → **44%**

        **This is Ethan's highest functional % this year.** Interesting
        to note: the "rainbow" exchange started as echoed fact but ended
        with a genuine self-correction. That pattern — echo first, then
        novel — is a developmental milestone we've been waiting for.

        **Goal G1 status: Approaching. Target is 50%. He's 6 points away.**
