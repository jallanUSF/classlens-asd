        # Anecdotal Note — Marcus Goes Down the Big Slide (04/10)

        **Student:** Marcus D.  
**Date:** 2026-04-10 (Friday)  
**Setting:** Kindergarten recess, playground  
**Observer:** Ms. Tran + Danny (1:1)  
**Related IEP Goals:** G3 (motor/sensory regulation) + informal milestone  

        ---

        **Context:** The "big slide" on the Kindergarten playground is the
        large curved tube slide. Marcus has been terrified of it all year.
        He'd previously only use the small straight slide. We'd been
        working on tolerance through:
        - Visual schedule with the big slide as a "choice"
        - Social story "Going Down the Big Slide"
        - Gradual exposure: touching the slide → sitting at the top →
          going down on a staff member's lap (3 weeks ago, cried)
        - Hearing his classmates talk about how fun it is

        **12:50** — Recess starts. Marcus heads to the small slide as
        usual. Goes down 4 times.

        **12:55** — Peer (Sam — same peer from Wednesday's pretend play
        observation) is going down the big slide repeatedly. Sam calls
        out "Marcus! Come with me!" Marcus stops, watches, doesn't move.

        **12:57** — Marcus walks over to the bottom of the big slide.
        Touches the slide. Looks up it. Walks back to the small slide.

        **1:00** — Marcus walks to the stairs of the big slide. Climbs
        3 steps. Stops. Comes back down.

        **1:02** — Marcus climbs the big slide stairs all the way to
        the top. Sam is already at the top. Sam: "Ready?" Marcus sits
        at the top. Looks down. 8-second pause. Danny is at the bottom
        giving a thumbs up. Marcus: [takes a deep breath — visibly —
        and pushes off]

        **1:02:08** — Marcus goes down the big slide. **By himself.**
        For the first time this school year.

        **1:02:12** — Marcus lands at the bottom. Sits there for 2
        seconds — quiet — processing. Then he SHRIEKS with laughter
        and says "AGAIN! AGAIN!"

        **1:02:30** — Goes up again. Goes down. Laughs. "BLUEY WOULD
        DO IT!"

        **1:05** — Goes down for the 3rd time.

        **1:08** — Goes down for the 6th time.

        **1:12** — Still going. Has stopped asking "again." Just cycling.

        **1:15** — Recess ends. Marcus doesn't want to line up. He wants
        to go down ONE MORE TIME. We let him. He slides, lands, runs to
        the line beaming.

        ---

        **This is not a data point against any specific IEP goal. This
        is a milestone moment for Marcus.**

        **What it represents:**
        - Motor regulation: Marcus self-regulated through a known
          aversive sensory experience (fast descent + vertigo)
        - Courage scaffolding: Used a peer model (Sam) + an internal
          model (Bluey) to get himself to "go"
        - Repair: He didn't get stuck in the fear loop. Once he'd
          broken through, the whole experience flipped — fear became
          joy within one slide cycle.

        **Email draft for parents is attached separately.** We want
        them to know this one. It's the kind of moment that makes the
        whole year of patient exposure work worth it.

        **Ms. Tran's personal note:** I cried a little. In a good way.
