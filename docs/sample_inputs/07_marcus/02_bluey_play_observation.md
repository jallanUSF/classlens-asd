        # Free Play Observation — Marcus (04/08)

        **Student:** Marcus D.  
**Date:** 2026-04-08  
**Setting:** Kindergarten free-choice / dramatic play corner  
**Observer:** Ms. Tran  
**Duration:** 18 minutes  
**Related IEP Goals:** G2 (pretend play with peer, 5+ min)  

        ---

        **Context:** Marcus is a Bluey superfan. The classroom has a few
        Bluey-adjacent props (blue and orange plush dogs, a toy mailbox,
        plastic "tea set" — all chosen to be Bluey-friendly). Marcus
        usually plays parallel (near peers but not WITH them). Today was
        different.

        **9:30** — Marcus walks to dramatic play corner. Picks up the
        blue plush and says "Bluey!" then the orange plush and says
        "Bingo!" [self-narration, parallel start]

        **9:31** — Peer (Sam) is already in the corner playing "restaurant."
        Sam: "Want to play restaurant?" Marcus: [doesn't answer. Continues
        with Bluey and Bingo.]

        **9:33** — Marcus starts acting out an episode — specifically
        "Keepy Uppy." He says "Keepy uppy! Don't let it touch the ground!"
        and bounces a soft ball up with the plush dog. Sam watches,
        puzzled.

        **9:35** — Sam: "Is that Bluey?" Marcus: "Yes. Keepy uppy."
        Sam: "Can I be Bingo?" Marcus: [pauses, looks at Sam, then
        hands him the orange plush] "Bingo keeps it up too." **[SHARED
        PLAY INITIATION — this is what we've been waiting for]**

        **9:36 – 9:41** — Sam and Marcus take turns bouncing the soft
        ball up with the plush dogs. Marcus narrates: "Bluey bounces!
        Bingo bounces! Don't let it touch the ground!" Sam matches the
        narration. They laugh twice. **[5 continuous minutes of
        collaborative pretend play — GOAL MET]**

        **9:41** — Ball rolls under a shelf. Marcus says "uh oh, game
        over." Sam: "No, we can reset. Reset!" Marcus: "Reset!" They
        start again with a new ball.

        **9:45** — Play shifts. Marcus gets the tea set out and says
        "Mum's making tea." (In Bluey, Mum is Chilli.) Sam plays along
        as "Dad" (Bandit). This is now imaginative play beyond the
        direct episode reenactment. **[EXTENSION of scripted play into
        novel territory — not tracked by the goal but a major dev win.]**

        **9:48** — Play ends when cleanup music starts. Marcus resists
        cleanup briefly (2 seconds) then says "all done" and puts Bluey
        away.

        ---

        **Goal metrics:**
        - **Continuous collaborative pretend play: 10 minutes**
          (target was 5 — exceeded 2x)
        - Play partners: 1 (Sam)
        - Adult prompts needed: 0
        - Transitions: 2 (keepy uppy → reset → tea party), both peer-led
          and accepted by Marcus

        **Goal G2 status: MET AND EXCEEDED.**

        **Qualitative note (Ms. Tran):** The magic moment was at 9:35 —
        Marcus handing the Bingo plush to Sam. A month ago he would have
        guarded both dogs. Today he actively *assigned a role*. The
        script from Bluey gave him a scaffold to invite a peer in. The
        peer-scripted social moment was Sam's "we can reset" — Marcus
        accepted a repair strategy he hadn't thought of.

        **Action item:** Let's make sure Sam is available during free-choice
        as often as possible. This pairing is working.
