## What ClassLens Does (Based on YOUR Ideas)

You gave us two ideas. We're building both of them:

1. **Your first idea** — Input a child's interest (dinosaurs, trains, space) + a learning target → get personalized lessons, activities, and worksheets themed around that interest. No more digging through TPT and Pinterest for 8 different kids.

2. **Your second idea** — Input each child's IEP goal → get lesson plans, activities that build toward that goal, AND data tracking sheets specific to that goal. Plus polished reports for admin.

ClassLens combines both: it reads student work photos, tracks IEP progress automatically, generates personalized lessons and materials themed to each kid's interests, and produces professional reports that administrators love.

**You are always the final decision-maker.** The AI drafts. You approve.

## Quick Validation Checklists

Tape these to your fridge or wherever you review things.

**For any AI output, ask:**
1. Does this reflect what I know about THIS specific student?
2. Is the vocabulary at the right level?
3. Would I use/submit/send this without major edits?
4. Does the format feel familiar and predictable (not surprising)?

**For lesson plans / activity ideas:**
1. Does this activity actually build toward the IEP goal?
2. Is it appropriate for this student's support level and communication level?
3. Are the student's interests incorporated naturally?
4. Could you realistically do this in your classroom with your resources?
5. Is it scaffolded appropriately (not too easy, not too hard)?

**For data tracking sheets:**
1. Can you fill this out while teaching (not just after)?
2. Are the right things being tracked for this goal?
3. Is the layout intuitive — could a para use it without explanation?
4. Does it match how IEP goals are actually measured (trials, percentages, frequency)?

**For social stories:**
1. First person? ("I" not "Maya")
2. More descriptive/affirmative sentences than directive ones?
3. Student's interests woven in naturally (not forced)?
4. Concrete vocabulary (no metaphors, no idioms)?

**For progress notes:**
1. Correct IEP goal referenced?
2. Trial data accurate?
3. Trend direction noted?
4. Professional enough for the IEP file?

**For admin reports:**
1. Would this impress your principal or program supervisor?
2. Is the data presented clearly with trends visible?
3. Is the language appropriate for a non-classroom audience?
4. Would you bring this to an IEP meeting?

**For parent communications:**
1. Does it reference something specific from today?
2. Does it celebrate something concrete?
3. Does it suggest ONE thing the parent can do at home?
4. Is the tone warm but professional?
5. Would you actually send this home?
